Follow Basecamp/Hey.com/Fizzy.do tone: direct, human, opinionated, talks to the owner not the enterprise.

**Why:** The product is built for SMB owners who are skeptical of startup marketing language. Generic or corporate copy loses them immediately. Peer-level directness earns trust.

**Banned words/phrases:** leverage, empower, streamline, unlock, seamless, game-changer, "We'd love to hear from you", "How can we help?", "Get started"

**CTA style:** Specific and low-friction. "Audit your invoicing process — free" beats "Get started". "Start your free audit" beats "Request a Demo".

**How to apply:**
- Every H1 should lead with the owner's pain or a direct, opinionated statement — not a feature or product name
- Value prop must be clear in the first two sentences of any section
- Use dollar amounts and time units to make ROI concrete, not abstract
- FAQ questions should sound like real skeptical questions, not softballs
- Contact/utility pages should still feel human — avoid "Contact Us" as H1, avoid support-bot language
